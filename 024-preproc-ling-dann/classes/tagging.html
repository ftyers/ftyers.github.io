Hand-annotating parts of speech

Tagging text is the task of assigning a part-of-speech tag for each of the tokens in a sentence by hand. For example, suppose we had the sentence: "В том институте все уехали.", we would first tokenise it using our tokeniser: 

# sent_id = 1
# text = В том институте все уехали.
1	В	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_
2	том	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_
3	институте	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_
4	все	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_
5	уехали	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_
6	.	_	_	_	_	_	_	_	_

Then we would go through the text line by line, and write a part-of-speech tag in the fourth column of every line. The part-of-speech tags are from the Universal Dependencies project and can be found with full definitions here.

Here is a brief overview:
Open class words Closed class words Other
ADJ (Adjective) ADP (Adposition) PUNCT (Punctuation)
ADV (Adverb) AUX (Auxiliary) SYM (Symbol)
INTJ (Interjection) CCONJ (Co-ordinating conjunction) X (Other)
NOUN (Noun) DET (Determiner)  
PROPN (Proper noun) NUM (Numeral)  
VERB (Verb) PART («Particle»)  
  PRON (Pronoun)  
  SCONJ (Subordinating conjunction)  
The final result should look something like this: 

# sent_id = 1
# text = В том институте все уехали.
1	В	_	ADP	_	_	_	_	_	_
2	том	_	DET	_	_	_	_	_	_
3	институте	_	NOUN	_	_	_	_	_	_
4	все	_	PRON	_	_	_	_	_	_
5	уехали	_	VERB	_	_	_	_	_	_
6	.	_	PUNCT	_	_	_	_	_	_

If you would like a graphical interface for adding the tags, I can recommend UD Annotatrix, which can be tried out online here. Note that your CoNLL-U should we well formed if you are planning to use the interface!

You should annotate at least 10 sentences of text, or 100 tokens, whichever is lower.
